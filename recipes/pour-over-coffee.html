Pour-over Coffee

Chop wood, carry water.

Description

By collecting and grinding our own beans, then heating and pouring water we can bring zen to our daily coffee routine.

Implements

  • Food scale, set to grams
  • Coffee grinder
  • Coffee dripper
  • Filter for dripper
  • Kettle to heat water (prefer goose-neck for precise pouring
  • 12 ounce or larger heat-resistant mug or flask

Ingredients

  • 23 grams light-roast, whole bean coffee (prefer single-origin)
  • 400 grams organically filtered water (prefer glacier water if not too dear)

Steps

  1. Heat water in kettle to 203 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Grind coffee beans at roughly the fineness of table salt
  3. Place filter in dripper and set on top of mug or flask
  4. Place coffee grounds in filter
  5. Slowly and evenly pour 50 grams of water over the grounds
  6. Let steep for 30 seconds
  7. Slowly and evenly pour another 100 grams of wanter over the grounds
  8. Pause for water to recede to within 1 centimeter of top of grounds
  9. Repeat steps 7-8 until total water poured reaches 350 grams
  10. Let water complete filtering through grounds then dispose of the filter and grounds (or set aside for compost)
  11. Sip and enjoy with appropriate levels of biological heat-sensing wisdom
